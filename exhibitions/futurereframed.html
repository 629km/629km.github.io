Future Reframed

Speculative design installation envisioning everyday life after the “future singularity.” Narrative prototypes, systems maps, and interaction dioramas frame plausible, preferable, and precautionary futures.

Overview

Two Visions About the Future: Future Reframed is an exhibition that interprets future design through the perspectives of two industrial designers. The exhibition divides the future into two contrasting characteristics and visualizes them through product design, space, scenarios, and objects. “Hyperism” explores the convergence of technology and artificial intelligence, focusing on rapid societal change and innovation in future systems. “Post-Minimalism” loosens the boundaries between design and art, emphasizing emotional and tactile elements to imagine a more sensorial future. The exhibition expands perspectives by utilizing images, AI, and various media. Through two contrasting future narratives, viewers can compare and reconsider the evolving role and potential of design in a rapidly changing era.
