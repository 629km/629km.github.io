Overview

Special Disaster Response Mobile Hospital is an inclusive design case developed to ensure that no one is excluded from receiving medical care, even during disasters. This mobile hospital responds to various special disasters, such as war, natural disasters, and infectious diseases, while guaranteeing safe and equal access to healthcare for all, including the elderly, persons with disabilities, and isolated individuals. Its modular structure allows flexible expansion and rapid deployment, enabling effective medical support even in urban outskirts or areas with limited infrastructure. Equipped for isolation and emergency care, it also considers physical and psychological safety in infectious disease situations. Rather than a simple structure, it functions as adaptive infrastructure that can respond swiftly to changing conditions. It is designed to be accessible regardless of gender, age, physical condition, or illness, reflecting an empathy-based design philosophy that minimizes both physical and psychological barriers. Ultimately, this project embodies medical equity and dignified recovery, offering a new standard for public architecture that realizes the values of inclusion and solidarity beyond safety.
